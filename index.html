Completed: December 27, 2025
Argumentative Prompt: To what extent do teen films reinforce harmful beauty standards and gender expectations?
Thesis: Although presented as a harmless romantic comedy, She's All That reinforces damaging social hierarchies by equating physical transformation with worth, portraying popularity as success, and positioning male approval as the ultimate validation for women.
Evidence:
  • The prom-queen bet reduces Laney to a social experiment, showing that social status can be manipulated through appearance rather than personal character.
  • The makeover sequence (removing glasses, changing clothing) implies that social acceptance is contingent on conforming to conventional beauty standards.
  • Mean Girls demonstrates how social value among adolescents is dictated by rigid popularity hierarchies, with attractiveness, social alliances, and conformity shaping who is accepted. The “Plastics” function as a symbolic representation of cultural pressure to perform beauty and social norms, showing that identity and worth are socially constructed rather than intrinsic.
  • The Princess Diaries (2001), where Mia’s makeover (hair, clothes, posture) is what enables her to be publicly recognized as a princess, emphasizing that appearance validates social worth.
